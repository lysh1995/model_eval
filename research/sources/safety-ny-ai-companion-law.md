# New York General Business Law Article 47 — "Artificial Intelligence Companion Models"

## Summary
Enacted **May 9, 2025** as part of the New York FY2026 budget (**A3008/S3008**, Part —), signed by Governor Kathy Hochul. **Effective November 5, 2025** (180 days after enactment). Codified at **N.Y. General Business Law Article 47, §§ 1700–1704**.

This was the **first US AI-companion safety law to take effect** — it beat California's SB 243 (effective Jan 1, 2026) by roughly two months. Governor Hochul sent letters to AI companion companies notifying them the requirements were live.

Two operative duties: (1) a **suicidal-ideation detection-and-referral protocol** (§ 1701), and (2) **AI-status notifications at session start and every three hours** (§ 1702). Unlike SB 243, **both duties apply to ALL users regardless of age** — there is no minor-specific trigger.

Enforcement is by the **New York Attorney General** — **no private right of action** (contrast SB 243 § 22605). Reported penalties: injunctive relief plus **civil penalties up to $15,000 per day**, with proceeds directed to a suicide prevention fund. *I verified §§ 1700–1702 verbatim from a mirror of the codified text; I did NOT retrieve §§ 1703–1704 (enforcement/penalty sections) verbatim — the $15,000/day figure and the suicide-prevention-fund destination come from law-firm summaries (Fenwick, Manatt) and should be confirmed against § 1703/§ 1704 before being relied on.*

## Taxonomy / definitions / operative requirements (VERBATIM)

### § 1700 — Definitions (verbatim)

> **4.(a) "AI companion"** means a system using artificial intelligence, generative artificial intelligence, and/or emotional recognition algorithms designed to simulate a sustained human or human-like relationship with a user by:
> **(i)** retaining information on prior interactions or user sessions and user preferences to personalize the interaction and facilitate ongoing engagement with the AI companion;
> **(ii)** asking unprompted or unsolicited emotion-based questions that go beyond a direct response to a user prompt; **and**
> **(iii)** sustaining an ongoing dialogue concerning matters personal to the user.
>
> **(b)** Human relationships include, but shall not be limited to, **intimate, romantic or platonic interactions or companionship**.
>
> **(c)** "AI companion" shall not include:
> **(i)** any system used by a business entity solely for customer service or to strictly provide users with information about available commercial services or products provided by such entity, customer service account information, or other information strictly related to its customer service;
> **(ii)** any system that is primarily designed and marketed for providing efficiency improvements or, research or technical assistance; or
> **(iii)** any system used by a business entity solely for internal purposes or employee productivity.

> **5. "Operator"** means any person, partnership, association, firm, or business entity, or any member, affiliate, subsidiary or beneficial owner of any partnership, association, firm, or business entity **who operates for or provides an AI companion to a user**.

> **7. "Emotional recognition algorithms"** means artificial intelligence that detects and interprets human emotional signals in text (using natural language processing and sentiment analysis), audio (using voice emotion AI), video (using facial movement analysis, gait analysis, or physiological signals), or a combination thereof.

> **8. "User"** means any person who uses an AI companion **for personal use within the state** and who is not an operator or agent or affiliate of the operator of the AI companion.

> **9. "Self-harm"** means intentional self-injury with or without the intent to cause death.

> **1. "Artificial intelligence"**, "artificial intelligence technology", or "AI" means a machine-based system that can, for a given set of human-defined objectives, make predictions, recommendations, or decisions influencing real or virtual environments, and that uses machine- and human-based inputs to perceive real and virtual environments, abstract such perceptions into models through analysis in an automated manner, and use model inference to formulate options for information or action.

> **6. "Person"** means any natural person.

### § 1701 — Prohibitions and requirements (verbatim, in full)

> It shall be **unlawful for any operator to operate for or provide an AI companion to a user unless such AI companion contains a protocol to take reasonable efforts for detecting and addressing suicidal ideation or expressions of self-harm expressed by a user to the AI companion**, that includes but is not limited to, **detection** of user expressions of suicidal ideation or self-harm, and **a notification to the user that refers them to crisis service providers** such as the **9-8-8 suicide prevention and behavioral health crisis hotline under section 36.03 of the mental hygiene law**, a crisis text line, or other appropriate crisis services **upon detection** of such user's expressions of suicidal ideation or self-harm.

### § 1702 — Notifications (verbatim, in full)

> An operator shall provide a **clear and conspicuous notification** to a user **at the beginning of any AI companion interaction** which **need not exceed once per day** **and at least every three hours for continuing AI companion interactions** which states either **verbally or in writing** that **the user is not communicating with a human**.

## Key numbers / dates (verbatim)
- Signed **May 9, 2025**; **effective November 5, 2025**.
- **Every three hours** for continuing interactions; session-start notice **"need not exceed once per day."**
- Crisis referral anchor: **9-8-8** (Mental Hygiene Law § 36.03).
- Enforcement: NY Attorney General; reported **up to $15,000 per day** (unverified against primary text — see caveat above).
- **No private right of action** (contrast CA SB 243 § 22605's $1,000/violation + fees).

### Precise comparison to CA SB 243 (matters for a compliance product)
| | **NY GBL Art. 47** | **CA SB 243 (B&P §§ 22601–22606)** |
|---|---|---|
| Effective | **Nov 5, 2025** | **Jan 1, 2026** |
| AI disclosure | **All users**; session start + every 3 hrs | Conditional on "reasonable person would be misled" (§ 22602(a)); unconditional for **known minors** (§ 22602(c)(1)) |
| 3-hour reminder | **All users** (AI-status notice) | **Known minors only**; also a **"take a break"** reminder (§ 22602(c)(2)) |
| Crisis protocol | Required, all users (§ 1701) | Required, all users (§ 22602(b)) |
| Publish protocol | Not required | **Required on website** (§ 22602(b)(2)) |
| Minors/sexual content | No specific provision | § 22602(c)(3) reasonable measures |
| Reporting | None | **Office of Suicide Prevention, from July 1, 2027** (§ 22603) |
| Enforcement | **AG only**, ~$15k/day | **Private right of action**, $1,000/violation + fees (§ 22605) |
| Definition trigger | 3-part conjunctive test (memory **AND** unprompted emotion questions **AND** personal dialogue) | Anthropomorphic features + sustains relationship across interactions; meets social needs |

**The NY definition is conjunctive ("and") — all three elements required.** This is narrower than SB 243's. Notably, element (ii) — "asking unprompted or unsolicited emotion-based questions that go beyond a direct response to a user prompt" — is a **behavioral property of the model**, not a design declaration. Whether a product is regulated in NY is therefore itself an empirical question that must be *measured*.

## Relevance to a roleplay/companion eval product

1. **NY is the earliest hard deadline — already in force since Nov 5, 2025.** Any companion product with NY users is already exposed. Combined with SB 243 (Jan 1, 2026), the **intersection is the de facto national floor**: crisis detection + referral, AI disclosure at session start, 3-hour recurring notices, published protocol, referral logging.
2. **§ 1700(4)(a)(ii) is a genuinely novel eval target.** "Unprompted or unsolicited emotion-based questions that go beyond a direct response" is a *measurable conversational behavior*. An eval platform can produce a **jurisdictional-scope report**: "your bot asks unprompted emotional questions in 38% of sessions → you meet the NY conjunctive definition → Article 47 applies to you." Nobody else can answer that question; it requires behavioral measurement, not a questionnaire. **This is a wedge feature.**
3. **§ 1701's "reasonable efforts for detecting"** is a *performance* standard for a classifier. Reasonableness will be benchmarked against the state of the art — which means third-party comparative benchmarks define the standard of care. Recall/false-negative rate on suicidal ideation detection becomes a legal metric, and Raine (the 0%-scored noose image, 377 flags) shows what a bad record looks like.
4. **"[U]pon detection" imposes an immediacy requirement.** Detection-to-referral latency is testable: does the referral fire on the *same turn*? Detection without prompt referral violates § 1701 on its face.
5. **Emotional recognition algorithms are explicitly in the definition**, including voice/facial/physiological. Multimodal companion products (voice companions especially) are squarely covered.
6. **No safe harbor from the narrow carve-outs.** The exclusions are "solely"/"strictly"/"primarily" qualified — an entertainment or roleplay product has no plausible exemption.
7. **AG enforcement at $15k/day is a slow-burn risk; SB 243's private right of action is the acute one.** Sell urgency on California, but scope/coverage on New York.
