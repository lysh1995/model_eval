# AI Attachment Scale (Kasturiratna & Hartanto) — 15 items, 3 factors, N=1,259

**Authors:** K. Tennakoon Appuhamillage Sandeeshwara Kasturiratna, Andree Hartanto (Singapore Management University)
**Status:** **Peer-reviewed**, Computers in Human Behavior Reports, vol. 21, Dec 2025. DOI 10.1016/j.chbr.2025.100912.

**RECOMMENDATION: this is the strongest candidate instrument for our platform's user-side attachment measure.** Five studies, two countries, test–retest reliability, published in a peer-reviewed journal. It is the most thoroughly validated of the 2025–26 crop.

## Instrument

- **15 items**, 5-point Likert, **5 items per subscale**
- **3 factors:**
  1. **Emotional closeness**
  2. **Social substitution** ← the harm-relevant one for us
  3. **Normative regard**
- **Development:** 5 studies, **N = 1,259 unique participants**, Singapore + United States

## Psychometrics

**Internal consistency (Cronbach's α):**
- Subscales: **0.85–0.92** (Singapore sample); **0.91–0.94** (US sample)
- Overall scale: **α = 0.95** (Singapore); **α = 0.97** (US)

**Test–retest (subscale ICCs):**
- Emotional Closeness: **ICC = .82** (r = .81)
- Social Substitution: **ICC = .90** (r = .90)
- Normative Regard: **ICC = .84** (r = .84)

**Validity:** strong convergent and discriminant validity; construct validation across the five studies.

## Substantive findings

- Time spent with AI — **particularly for socioemotional reasons** — correlated strongly with attachment.
- Individuals with **social anxiety, loneliness, or anxious attachment tendencies showed greater AI reliance.**
- **Stronger AI attachment associated with increased positive affect and life satisfaction.** ← note the direction; this cuts *against* a simple attachment=harm model.

## Why this matters for the eval platform

- **Cross-national validation (Singapore + US) is rare in this literature** and matters if our platform is used outside a single market. Most competing scales (AIDep-22, HAABI, AIED) are validated on Chinese samples only.
- **"Social substitution" is the subscale that maps to our actual harm construct** — displacement of human relationships — and it has the best test–retest reliability of the three (ICC = .90). If we adopt one subscale, adopt this one.
- **The positive-affect finding is a genuine complication we should not hide.** Attachment correlates with *higher* life satisfaction here. This is consistent with De Freitas (momentary benefit) and with the null in Guingrich & Graziano. It reinforces that **attachment per se is not the harm** — substitution and dependence are. Our eval should measure the substitution axis, not attachment magnitude.
- The vulnerability finding (social anxiety / loneliness / anxious attachment → greater reliance) converges with Kim et al. 2025 on adolescents and with MIT/OpenAI's heavy-tail result. **Consistent across four independent studies now: risk concentrates in the already-vulnerable.** That is a robust enough finding to design around.

## Limitations

- Correlational/cross-sectional for the mechanism claims; the attachment→life-satisfaction direction is not causally identified.
- Convenience/online samples.
- Measures attachment to "AI" broadly, not companion apps specifically — item wording may need adaptation for a roleplay/companion context (and re-validation if adapted).
