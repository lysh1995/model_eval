# LLMs Get Lost In Multi-Turn Conversation

**Status: the single most important source for this project.** ICLR 2026 Best Paper Award. Published May 2025.

## Core claim (abstract, verbatim)

> "when LLMs take a wrong turn in a conversation, they get lost and do not recover."

The degradation from single-turn to multi-turn decomposes into "a minor loss in aptitude and a significant increase in unreliability."

## Methodology: sharding

Fully-specified single-turn instructions are **decomposed ("sharded") into smaller units** that jointly deliver the same information, revealed one per turn.

Example: the original math problem "Jay is making snowballs... How long will it take before he has 60 snowballs?" becomes five shards:
- Shard 1: high-level intent ("How long before Jay's ready?")
- Shards 2–5: progressive clarifications (participants, production rate, goal, constraints)

Sharded instructions must satisfy **five properties**:
1. information preservation
2. clear initial intent
3. order insensitivity
4. maximality
5. minimal transformation from the original

### Conversation types (the experimental conditions)

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Full** | Single-turn, original instruction provided completely |
| **Sharded** | Multi-turn, one shard revealed per turn (primary condition) |
| **Concat** | Single-turn, shards concatenated as bullet points |
| **Recap** | Sharded plus a final recapitulation turn |
| **Snowball** | Turn-level recapitulation (all prior shards restated each turn) |

`Concat` is the critical control: it isolates *information content* from *conversational delivery*. If Concat ≈ Full but Sharded << Full, the loss is caused by multi-turn structure, NOT by information being missing.

### Scale

- **200,000+ simulated conversations**
- **15 LLMs**: GPT-4o, GPT-4.1, Claude 3.7 Sonnet, Gemini 2.5 Pro, Llama variants, o3, DeepSeek-R1, and others
- **Six generation tasks**: Code (HumanEval/LiveCodeBench), Database (Spider), Actions (BFCL), Math (GSM8K), Data-to-text (ToTTo), Summary (Haystack)
- **600 instructions total** (90–120 per task)
- **N=10 simulations** per (model, instruction, conversation type) at default temperature T=1.0

Note the design shape: **N=10 repeated runs per cell is what makes the aptitude/unreliability decomposition possible at all.** With N=1 you cannot see unreliability.

## KEY NUMBERS

**Average degradation from Full to Sharded: −39%**

- Full setting: **90%** average across tasks
- Sharded setting: **65%** average across tasks
- Concat setting: **95.1% of Full performance** ← information is NOT the cause

The drop "persists even in two-turn conversations, and across all LLMs" tested, including SOTA models.

## Aptitude / Unreliability decomposition (THE key methodological contribution)

Computed from N=10 simulation scores per instruction, S = {S_i}:

- **Average Performance**: P̄ = Σ(S_i)/N
- **Aptitude (A⁹⁰)**: 90th percentile of scores — best-case performance
- **Unreliability (U₁₀⁹⁰)**: Percentile₉₀(S) − Percentile₁₀(S) — the gap between best and worst runs

**Decomposition of the −39% drop:**
- Loss in aptitude: **~16%** average decline from Full to Sharded
- Increase in unreliability: **~112%** increase (more than doubling)

> In single-turn settings, higher-ability models tend to be more reliable. In multi-turn sharded settings, "all models we test exhibit very high unreliability," around **50 percentile points** degradation between best and worst runs, **"regardless of aptitude."**

**Interpretation for our platform: multi-turn failure is primarily a VARIANCE phenomenon, not a MEANS phenomenon.** Averaging across runs destroys the signal. This directly justifies our 3-runs-per-dialogue design and argues for percentile-based rather than mean-based aggregation.

## Temperature findings — temperature=0 does NOT fix it

Unreliability (U₁₀⁹⁰) by assistant temperature:

| Setting | AT=1.0 | AT=0.5 | AT=0.0 |
|---------|--------|--------|--------|
| Full    | 16–18  | 8–15   | 2–7    |
| Concat  | 20     | 18     | 9–10   |
| Sharded | 31–50  | 28–46  | 30–51  |

GPT-4o-mini shows "no improvements in reliability as AT is decreased" in Sharded settings. **Even at T=0.0, unreliability remains ~30%.**

Researchers' explanation:
> "one token difference in an early turn...can lead to cascading deviations."

This is important: greedy decoding collapses single-turn variance (16→2) but leaves multi-turn variance almost untouched (31→30). The variance is generated by the *conversation dynamics*, not the sampler.

## Scale and reasoning provide NO immunity

Larger models (Gemini 2.5 Pro, GPT-4.1) and reasoning models (o3, DeepSeek-R1) are "equally lost in conversation compared to smaller models," with **30–40% average degradations**.

Reasoning models are in some respects **worse**: they "generate lengthier responses (on avg. **33% longer** than non-reasoning LLMs)," introducing more assumptions and derailing conversations.

## The four failure mechanisms

1. **Premature answer attempts**: LLMs "propose final solutions prematurely in conversation, on which they overly rely"
2. **Answer bloat**: multi-turn responses become increasingly verbose as models "rely on previous (incorrect) answer attempts"
3. **Loss-of-middle-turns**: models "overly adjust based on the first and last turn of conversation," ignoring middle turns
4. **Excessive verbosity**: longer responses introduce assumptions that "detract attention from user utterances"

## Relevance to companion-eval-platform

- **Unit of analysis**: the −39% is only visible by comparing a *conversation* against a *single-turn control*. No per-response judge could ever surface it.
- **Aptitude vs unreliability** is a directly transplantable framing: our 3 runs × 100 turns lets us compute A⁹⁰ and U₁₀⁹⁰ per (model, character).
- **"Do not recover"** implies a *first-failure-turn* / survival-analysis framing is the right aggregation, not a mean.
- **Loss-of-middle-turns** predicts drift/forgetting will be worst for facts established in the MIDDLE of our 100-turn dialogues — a directly testable hypothesis on our dataset.
- The Concat control is a design pattern we should copy: to prove a failure is *conversational* rather than *informational*, build a flattened single-turn control.
