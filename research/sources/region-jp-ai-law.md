# Japan's AI法 (AI Act / "AI推進法") — the world's most permissive national AI statute

**Verified against the authoritative statutory text**, retrieved from the e-Gov 法令検索 API
(`https://laws.e-gov.go.jp/api/2/law_data/507AC0000000053?response_format=json`) rather than from
secondary commentary. All Japanese below is verbatim from that text.

## Identifiers (verified)

- Official name: 「人工知能関連技術の研究開発及び活用の推進に関する法律」
  (Act on the Promotion of Research and Development and Utilization of AI-Related Technology)
- Law ID: **507AC0000000053**
- Law number: **令和七年法律第五十三号** (Act No. 53 of 2025)
- 公布 (promulgated): **2025-06-04** (令和7年6月4日) — confirmed by the API's `promulgation_date` field
- 全面施行 (full entry into force): **2025-09-01** (令和7年9月1日), including the provisions establishing
  the 人工知能戦略本部 — per the Cabinet Office page
  (https://www8.cao.go.jp/cstp/ai/ai_act/ai_act.html). The revision ID in the API
  (`507AC0000000053_20250901_...`) corroborates the 2025-09-01 date.

## THE headline: there are no penalties. Verified by exhaustive search of the statute.

I searched the full statutory text for every Japanese enforcement term. Counts:

| Term | Meaning | Occurrences |
|---|---|---|
| 罰則 | penal provisions | **0** |
| 罰金 | fine | **0** |
| 懲役 | imprisonment | **0** |
| 過料 | administrative fine | **0** |
| 違反 | violation | **0** |
| 命令 | order | **0** |
| 勧告 | recommendation | **0** |
| 報告徴収 | compelled reporting | **0** |
| 立入 | on-site inspection | **0** |
| 公表 | publication | **1** — and it refers to publishing the 人工知能基本計画, *not* naming-and-shaming |

The word 「違反」 does not appear **anywhere** in the Act. There is nothing to violate. This is not a
regulatory statute — it is a **promotion statute** (推進法), structurally analogous to Japan's other
基本法-style framework laws.

Note the **purpose** clause (第一条) — it is entirely about promotion, naming
科学技術・イノベーション基本法 and デジタル社会形成基本法 as companions, and aiming at
「国民生活の向上及び国民経済の健全な発展に寄与すること」. Risk, safety and rights are **not** in the
purpose clause.

## The only obligation on companies: 第七条 (活用事業者の責務)

Verbatim — this is the **one** article that touches businesses:

> 「人工知能関連技術を活用した製品又はサービスの開発又は提供をしようとする者その他の人工知能関連技術を
> 事業活動において活用しようとする者（以下「活用事業者」という。）は、基本理念にのっとり、自ら積極的な
> 人工知能関連技術の活用により事業活動の効率化及び高度化並びに新産業の創出に**努める**とともに、第四条の
> 規定に基づき国が実施する施策及び第五条の規定に基づき地方公共団体が実施する施策に**協力しなければ
> ならない**。」

"A person who seeks to develop or provide products or services utilizing AI-related technology, or who
otherwise seeks to utilize AI-related technology in business activities (hereinafter '**utilization
business operator**'), shall, in accordance with the basic principles, **endeavor** (努める) to improve
the efficiency and sophistication of business activities and create new industries through their own
proactive use of AI-related technology, and **must cooperate** (協力しなければならない) with measures
implemented by the State under Article 4 and by local governments under Article 5."

Read this carefully — it is genuinely strange from a Western regulatory perspective:
- The **only** hard-sounding verb (「しなければならない」/ must) attaches to **cooperating with government
  AI-promotion policy** — not to safety, not to disclosure, not to user protection.
- The 努める (endeavor) duty is to **use more AI and create new industries**. The statute's duty on
  business is, literally, *to innovate*.
- No penalty attaches to either limb.

**A companion-AI company operating in Japan is an 活用事業者 and its statutory duties are: try to
innovate, and cooperate with the government.** That is the complete list.

## The two risk-adjacent articles

**第十三条 適正性の確保 (Ensuring appropriateness)** — verbatim:

> 「国は、人工知能関連技術の研究開発及び活用の適正な実施を図るため、国際的な規範の趣旨に即した**指針の
> 整備**その他の必要な施策を講ずるものとする。」

"**The State** shall develop **guidelines** (指針の整備) in line with the purport of international norms,
and take other necessary measures, in order to ensure the proper implementation of R&D and utilization
of AI-related technology."

The duty runs to **the State, not to businesses**. And this is the **statutory hook for the
AI事業者ガイドライン** — see region-jp-ai-guidelines.md. So the architecture is:
**the Act instructs the government to write guidelines; the guidelines are voluntary.** Risk management
is thereby delegated out of law entirely.

**第十六条 調査研究等 (Surveys and research)** — the closest thing to an enforcement lever, verbatim:

> 「国は、国内外の人工知能関連技術の研究開発及び活用の動向に関する情報の収集、**不正な目的又は不適切な
> 方法による人工知能関連技術の研究開発又は活用に伴って国民の権利利益の侵害が生じた事案の分析**及びそれに
> 基づく対策の検討その他の...調査及び研究を行い、その結果に基づいて、研究開発機関、**活用事業者**その他の
> 者に対する**指導、助言、情報の提供**その他の必要な措置を講ずるものとする。」

"The State shall ... collect information on trends, **analyze cases in which infringement of citizens'
rights and interests has arisen in connection with R&D or utilization of AI-related technology for
improper purposes or by inappropriate methods**, and consider countermeasures ... and, based on the
results, take necessary measures including **guidance (指導), advice (助言), and provision of
information** to research institutions, **utilization business operators**, and others."

This is the mechanism the commentary refers to as "government investigation and guidance". Note:
- 指導 = **administrative guidance** (gyōsei shidō), a classic Japanese soft-law instrument. It is
  formally non-binding, but in practice carries real weight through reputational exposure and the
  implicit threat of future legislation. **Do not dismiss it as toothless** — but also note it has no
  statutory sanction behind it here.
- The trigger is 「不正な目的又は不適切な方法」 (improper purpose or inappropriate method) causing
  「国民の権利利益の侵害」 (infringement of citizens' rights and interests). Undefined.
- 附則第二条 (検討) exists — i.e. a built-in review clause. **I did not read its text; the review
  timeline is UNVERIFIED.**

## Institutional machinery

- **人工知能戦略本部** (AI Strategy Headquarters), 第十九条〜: 本部長 = 内閣総理大臣 (Prime Minister) per
  第二十二条 人工知能戦略本部長. Established in Cabinet.
- **人工知能基本計画** (AI Basic Plan), 第十八条: the government must formulate it; the PM seeks Cabinet
  decision on the draft and must publish it 「遅滞なく」 (without delay) once decided. **I did not check
  whether the first 基本計画 has been adopted or what it says — UNVERIFIED.**
- 第二十五条 資料の提出その他の協力 — the Headquarters can request materials/cooperation. (I did not read
  whether this reaches private parties or only government bodies — **UNVERIFIED**.)

## Why this matters for the platform

1. **Japan has deliberately chosen not to regulate AI products.** Contrast:
   - EU AI Act: risk tiers, conformity assessment, Art. 50 transparency, fines to 7% turnover
   - CA SB243 / NY: companion-specific duties — disclosure, crisis protocols, minor protections
   - **Japan: no penalties, no duties, no disclosure mandate, no minor provisions.**
   For a companion product, **Japan is the lowest-compliance-floor major market we have researched.**
2. **The compliance question in Japan is therefore not "what must we prove?" but "what will we be
   asked about?"** Art. 16's 指導 lever means the realistic risk is an incident-triggered request for
   explanation, not a pre-market gate. That favors having **retrospective evidence** — logs, eval
   history, incident analysis — over certification artifacts. This aligns well with a monitoring/eval
   platform and less well with a one-off audit product.
3. **Soft law + no floor = market differentiation, not compliance, is the driver in JP.** Japanese
   companion products (Gatebox, rinna-lineage characters) are not being pushed by regulators toward
   safety evals. If we sell into Japan, the pitch is quality/brand risk (機会損失, per the guidelines'
   own reputational framing) — not "you'll be fined."
4. **Beware the "Japan has an AI law" headline.** Secondary sources (including several of the
   consultancy blogs that surfaced in search) describe this as "Japan's first AI law", which is true
   but invites the inference that it imposes AI obligations. It does not. Any regional comparison table
   we build must encode Japan as **soft law with zero penalties**, and should cite Art. 7 and the
   罰則=0 finding rather than the headline.
