# Digital Omnibus on AI — timeline reset, and why it does NOT rescue a companion product

**Why this file exists**: there has been loud discussion of the AI Act being "delayed". For a companion
product, **the delay does not apply to the parts that bind you.** Art. 50 still lands 2 Aug 2026 and
Art. 5 has been in force since Feb 2025. Do not let "the AI Act got pushed back" become an internal
excuse to deprioritise the 2 Aug 2026 disclosure work — which is ~2.5 weeks out.

## Status as of 2026-07-16

- Commission proposal published **19 November 2025** (Commission digital-strategy page; last updated
  19 Jan 2026).
- **Provisional political agreement 6 May 2026**; confirmed by Member State representatives **13 May
  2026** (Gibson Dunn). Council/Parliament announced agreement **7 May 2026** (Consilium press
  release, 403'd on fetch — see Unverified below).
- **European Parliament vote: 16 June 2026 — approved 423 in favour / 57 against / 174 abstentions.**
  (Vote tally reported consistently across multiple outlets incl. AGENCE EUROPE, BEUC, iubenda,
  Sofia Globe.) **Council final adoption: 29 June 2026.** (Freshfields; consistent with a Consilium
  press release dated 29 June 2026 titled "Council gives final green light", which I could not fetch
  — 403.)
- **Entry into force: third day after OJ publication, expected July 2026.**
- **Confidence note**: the 16 June Parliament vote is corroborated across four+ independent sources
  including a vote tally, so it is high confidence. The 29 June Council adoption is corroborated by a

## What CHANGED

| Obligation | Was | Now |
|---|---|---|
| High-risk, stand-alone (Annex III) | 2 Aug 2026 | **2 Dec 2027** |
| High-risk, embedded in regulated products (Annex I) | 2 Aug 2027 | **2 Aug 2028** |
| Art. 50(2) machine-readable marking, for systems **already on market before 2 Aug 2026** | 2 Aug 2026 | **2 Dec 2026** (4-month grace) |
| Regulatory sandboxes | — | 2 Aug 2027 (per Freshfields) |

**New Art. 5 prohibition added**: AI systems generating/manipulating **CSAM** and **non-consensual
sexual or intimate content** ("nudifiers"). Placing on market **and** use prohibited. Technical
safeguards / transitional period to **2 Dec 2026**. See `region-eu-ai-act-art5-manipulation.md` — this
is a *new* top-tier (7%) exposure aimed at exactly the content category a romantic companion operates in.

## What did NOT change — the important part

- **Art. 50 transparency obligations still apply from 2 August 2026.** Confirmed independently by
  Gibson Dunn, Freshfields, and Latham & Watkins. The user's belief is correct. The only Art. 50
  softening is the narrow 50(2) marking grace period above, which is about **watermarking synthetic
  output**, not about telling users they're talking to an AI.
- **Art. 5 prohibitions still in force since 2 February 2025**, unmodified (and now *extended* by the
  new CSAM/NCII prohibition).
- **Art. 99 penalty tiers unchanged** — Art. 5 at EUR 35M/7%, Art. 50 at EUR 15M/3%. **UNVERIFIED**
  that the Omnibus leaves Art. 99 untouched; none of the three sources mentioned changes to penalties,
  but absence of mention is not confirmation.

## Net effect for a companion product

**The Omnibus is close to irrelevant to this product's exposure, and slightly negative on net.**

1. The high-risk delay is the headline, but a companion product is **not** Annex III high-risk in the
   ordinary case — so the relief doesn't reach you. (**UNVERIFIED**: I did not walk the Annex III list
   this pass. Worth a check — an emotion-inference or minor-facing feature could conceivably touch a
   category. Default assumption: not high-risk, transparency-risk only.)
2. Art. 50 lands **2 Aug 2026** regardless. ~2.5 weeks from today.
3. Art. 5(1)(b) has been live for 17 months already.
4. The Omnibus **adds** a prohibition squarely aimed at sexual content generation.

**So: the "AI Act is delayed" narrative is true for HR-screening and credit-scoring vendors, and false
for AI companions.** If anything the Omnibus made the companion picture marginally worse by adding a
7%-tier prohibition on non-consensual sexual content generation with a 2 Dec 2026 deadline.
