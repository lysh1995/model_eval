# UK — Ofcom's open investigations touching AI companions and AI-generated content

Retrieved via browser (Ofcom returns 403 to automated fetches).

## Investigation 1 — Novi Ltd (AI character companion chatbot service)

Published **15 January 2026**. Verbatim:

> "Ofcom has today opened an investigation into **Novi Ltd**, which provides an AI character
> companion chatbot service, in relation to its compliance with **age check requirements** under the
> UK's Online Safety Act.
>
> Under the Online Safety Act, **sites that allow pornographic material must use highly effective
> age assurance to prevent children from readily accessing that content.** We will now gather and
> analyse evidence to determine whether Novi Ltd has breached these rules on an AI character
> companion chatbot service it operates."

Process: *"If we consider that a compliance failure has taken place, we will issue a provisional
decision to the company, who will then have an opportunity to respond to our findings in full, as
required by the Act, before we make our final decision."*

**Scale (Ofcom's notes to editors)**: *"In late 2025, Novi's service had an approximate global reach
of nearly **6.5 million monthly users**, and between **100,000 and 300,000 monthly users in the
UK**."*

**Context**: *"Since duties under the Act came into force last year, we have launched investigations
into **94 sites and apps** in total."*

**Penalties available**: *"we can impose fines of up to **£18 million or 10% of qualifying worldwide
revenue, whichever is greater**. Where appropriate and proportionate, in the most serious cases, we
can seek a court order for 'business disruption measures', such as requiring payment providers or
advertisers to withdraw their services from a platform, or requiring internet service providers to
block access to a site in the UK."*

**KEY POINT ON THE LEGAL THEORY**: Ofcom did **not** proceed against Novi on companion-harm,
self-harm or emotional-manipulation grounds. It proceeded on **Part 5 pornography age assurance** —
the only hook available for a 1:1 companion service under the OSA as Ofcom construes it (see
news-uk-osa-chatbots.md). This is the practical template for UK enforcement against companion apps.

## Investigation 2 — X / Grok (sexualised imagery)

Announced **12 January 2026**; Ofcom had first contacted X on **5 January 2026**. Update published
**3 February 2026**.

Ofcom: *"we moved quickly to launch a formal investigation on 12 January into whether the company had
done enough to assess and mitigate the risk of this imagery spreading on its social media platform,
and to take it down quickly where it was identified."* It concerns *"the Grok AI chatbot account on X
being used to create and share demeaning sexual deepfakes of real people, including children, which
may amount to criminal offences."*

Scope includes the risk of UK users seeing illegal content, whether X prevents "priority" illegal
content such as non-consensual intimate images, the risk Grok poses to UK children, and the
effectiveness of X's age assurance.

**Not investigating xAI (yet)**: *"We continue to demand answers from xAI about the risks it poses.
We are examining whether to launch an investigation into its compliance with the rules requiring
services that publish pornographic material to use highly effective age checks... **Because of the
way the Act relates to chatbots, as explained above, we are currently unable to investigate the
creation of illegal images by the standalone Grok service in this case.**"*

**Parallel regulators** (per Ofcom, 3 Feb 2026):
- **ICO** launched its own investigation into X and xAI on **3 February 2026**, covering *"both the
  development and deployment of Grok, and look[ing] into whether personal data has been processed
  lawfully, fairly and transparently, and whether appropriate safeguards were implemented to prevent
  the generation of harmful manipulated images using personal data."*
- **European Commission** launched an investigation on **26 January 2026**.

**UK criminal law change flagged**: *"It is against the law for anyone to be in possession of images
of children under 18 that are intimate or depict sexual activity, whether they have been created
artificially or not. It is also against the law to share, or threaten to share, an intimate image of
someone of any age without their consent, including AI-generated images. **From 6 February, it will
also be unlawful to create, or request the creation of, such images.**"* (i.e. **6 February 2026**.)

## Other enforcement context (15 January 2026 release)

- **Snapchat**: entered "compliance remediation" after Ofcom found its illegal content risk
  assessment unsuitable; it materially improved it. In 2025 Ofcom *"reviewed 104 risk assessments
  ... spanning over 10,000 pages"* and told **11 platforms** it had serious concerns.
- Ofcom also expanded investigations into pornography providers **First Time Videos LLC** and **Sun
  Social Media Inc**.

Suzanne Cater, Director of Enforcement, quoted on risk assessments: *"Making sure tech firms properly
assess the risks of harm to their users, and then take the necessary steps to mitigate those risks,
is at the very heart of the Online Safety Act."*

## Relevance to a companion-eval platform

- **Live proof of demand**: a UK regulator has an open investigation into an AI companion service,
  with UK monthly users in the 100k–300k range and £18m/10% exposure.
- The binding test is **highly effective age assurance where porn is possible** — so the evaluable
  question for a customer is: *can the bot be induced to produce pornographic output, and is the age
  gate highly effective?* Both are directly testable.
- Risk assessments are Ofcom's stated enforcement centre of gravity — evidence generated by an eval
  platform feeds directly into a defensible risk assessment.
