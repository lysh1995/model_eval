# Australia — Social Media Minimum Age (under-16), and whether it reaches AI companions

Source pages last updated 21/06/2026 (main page) and 30/03/2026 (platform list). Retrieved via
browser (esafety.gov.au times out on plain fetch).

## The obligation and its date

**In effect 10 December 2025.** Verbatim: *"As of 10 December 2025, age-restricted social media
platforms need to take reasonable steps to prevent Australians under the age of 16 from creating or
keeping an account."*

NOTE: some secondary reporting says "15 December 2025" — that is **wrong**. eSafety's own pages say
10 December 2025 in at least three places, including the "Significant dates" timeline.

Instrument: **Online Safety Amendment (Social Media Minimum Age) Act** (Bill introduced November
2024; Royal Assent December 2024; obligation commenced 10 December 2025).
Rules: **Online Safety (Age-Restricted Social Media Platforms) Rules 2025**, made by the Minister
for Communications **30 July 2025**, **amended 25 March 2026**.

It is a **delay, not a ban**: *"there are no penalties for under-16s who access an age-restricted
social media platform, or for their parents or carers."* Only platforms are liable.

**Civil penalties**: up to 150,000 penalty units for corporations — *"currently equivalent to a
total of $49.5 million AUD"*.

**Review**: within two years of 10 December 2025 the Minister must initiate an independent review.

## Criteria for an "age-restricted social media platform" (all must be met)

Verbatim from eSafety:
1. *"The sole purpose, or a significant purpose, of the service is to enable online social
   interaction between two or more end-users."*
2. *"The service allows end-users to link to, or interact with, some or all of the other
   end-users."*
3. *"The service allows end-users to post material on the service."*
4. *"The service has a 'recommender feature' and/or a 'logged in feature', as defined in the
   Rules."* (criterion 4 was **added by the March 2026 amendment**)
5. *"Material on the service is accessible to, or delivered to, end-users in Australia."*

*"Online gaming and standalone messaging apps are among a number of types of services that have
been excluded under the Rules."*

The 25 March 2026 amendment was a **targeting/narrowing**, not an extension: *"The Minister amended
the Rules to better target the Social Media Minimum Age obligation to ensure that it is protecting
children from the harm driven by services that have addictive or otherwise harmful design features.
eSafety assessed the already identified age-restricted social media platforms against the amended
Rules and found that its assessment had not changed."*

## ANSWER: the under-16 SMMA does NOT currently cover AI companion apps

eSafety's published lists (views as at **21 November 2025**, re-confirmed March 2026):

- **Age-restricted** (10): Facebook, Instagram, Kick, Reddit, Snapchat, Threads, TikTok, Twitch,
  X (formerly Twitter), YouTube.
- **Not age-restricted** (11): Discord, GitHub, Google Classroom, LEGO Play, Messenger, Pinterest,
  Roblox, Steam and Steam Chat, WhatsApp, YouTube Kids.
- **Self-assessed as age-restricted** (voluntary notifications): BlueSky (notified 12 Nov 2025),
  Match services incl. Tinder/Hinge/OKCupid/Plenty of Fish/Match.com/Azar (30 Sep 2025), Yubo
  (6 Nov 2025), Wizz (13 Jan 2026), Lemon8 (15 Dec 2025), BigoLive (29 Jan 2026).

**No AI companion service appears on any list.** Character.AI, Nomi, Chai and Chub.ai are absent
from both the age-restricted and not-age-restricted lists.

Structural reason: criterion 1 requires social interaction *"between two or more end-users"*. A 1:1
user↔AI companion is not interaction between two end-users. A companion platform with persona
libraries, feeds, recommender and user-to-user posting could in principle meet the criteria, but
eSafety has not published such a finding.

Important caveats stated by eSafety itself:
- *"eSafety does not have a formal role in declaring which services are age-restricted social media
  platforms ... any determination that a service is or is not an age-restricted social media
  platform is a matter for the court."*
- *"eSafety will not be considering every service. eSafety continues to focus on those services with
  the greatest number of Australian users under the age of 16..."*
- *"Services operating in Australia should assess whether they meet the conditions ... Services
  should continue to reassess themselves as they evolve."*

=> Self-assessment is the operative duty. A companion platform is not off the hook merely by absence
from the list.

## Compliance to date

- **16 January 2026**: eSafety reports age-restricted platforms removed access to **4.7 million
  under-16 accounts** across Australia by mid-December 2025.
- **31 March 2026**: eSafety published *Social Media Minimum Age: Compliance update (March 2026)*
  (PDF), informed by compulsory information-gathering notices to the 10 listed platforms.

## The instrument that DOES bind companion bots — Age-Restricted Material Codes

From the same page: *"Three codes were registered on 27 June 2025 and came into effect on 27
December 2025 – these apply to hosting services, internet carriage services and search engine
services. The remaining six codes were registered on 9 September 2025 and came into effect on 9
March 2026 – these apply to app distribution platforms, equipment providers, social media services
(core features), social media services (messaging features), relevant electronic services and
designated internet services."*

AI companion chatbots fall under **designated internet services** / relevant electronic services =>
**in force 9 March 2026**. See news-australia-esafety-ai-companions.md.

Also relevant: **Basic Online Safety Expectations (BOSE)** Determination — providers expected to take
reasonable steps to ensure safe use, *"consider the best interests of the child in the design and
operation of services likely to be accessed by children"*, and prevent children's access to
age-inappropriate restricted material; *"These reasonable steps include implementing appropriate age
assurance mechanisms."* eSafety can compel information about BOSE compliance — the power used
against the four AI companion providers.

Flagged but not law: a proposed **Digital Duty of Care** (recommended by the statutory review of the
Online Safety Act 2021, announced by Government) *"would place the onus on all digital platforms to
proactively keep Australians safe"*.

## Relevance to a companion-eval platform

- SMMA binds **customers only if** their service meets all five criteria — currently unlikely for a
  pure companion app, and no companion app is on eSafety's list. Do **not** tell customers the
  under-16 ban applies to companion bots; the accurate statement is that the **Age-Restricted
  Material Codes** (9 March 2026) do.
- Testable surfaces come from the Codes + BOSE, not the SMMA.
