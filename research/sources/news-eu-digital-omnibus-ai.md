# EU — Digital Omnibus on AI: what it delayed, and what it did NOT

**Bottom line: Article 50(1) — the duty to disclose that a user is interacting with an AI — was NOT
delayed. It applies from 2 August 2026. The Omnibus delayed the HIGH-RISK regime, and granted a
narrow transitional window for Article 50(2) marking/detection only.**

This is the question the task flagged as commonly garbled in reporting. Three independent
confirmations below.

## Confirmation 1 — the Commission's own draft Art 50 guidelines, para (141)

From the 8 May 2026 draft guidelines PDF (see news-eu-ai-act-art50-guidance.md), verbatim:

> "According to Article 113 AI Act, Article 50 AI Act will apply as from 2 August 2026. This requires
> all in scope AI systems placed on the market or put into service in the Union to be compliant,
> regardless of their date of placement on the market or putting into service. The AI Omnibus
> proposal which is currently examined by the EU co-legislators envisages a targeted grandfathering
> rule **only with regard to the marking and detection obligations under Article 50(2)** for
> generative AI systems placed on the market or put into service before 2 August 2026, giving
> providers of those systems a transitional period to bring their systems in conformity."

## Confirmation 2 — European Parliament Legislative Train (page last updated 20 June 2026)

Records the deadline changes as:
- *"2 December 2027 for stand-alone high-risk AI systems, and 2 August 2028 for high-risk AI systems
  embedded in products"*
- AI-generated content marking obligation postponed to *"2 December 2026 for systems placed on the
  market before 2 August 2026"*

The page **makes no reference to any change to Article 50 transparency generally** — consistent with
Art 50(1) being untouched.

## Confirmation 3 — Council press releases

- 7 May 2026 provisional agreement; 29 June 2026 final Council green light (press release:
  *"Artificial Intelligence: Council gives final green light to simplify and streamline rules"*).
  Council reports a grace period for systems on the market before 2 Aug 2026, which *"must comply
  with the marking and detection obligations by 2 December 2026"*.

NOTE — a minor discrepancy in secondary reporting: some sources call the Art 50(2) window a
"four-month grace period", one Council-derived summary calls it "three months". Both agree the
endpoint is **2 December 2026**. 2 August → 2 December is four months. The endpoint is what matters
and it is consistent across sources.

## What the Omnibus changed (verified)

1. **High-risk applicability deferred**: Annex III stand-alone high-risk → **2 December 2027**;
   Annex I high-risk embedded in regulated products → **2 August 2028**.
2. **Art 50(2) marking/detection grandfathering**: generative AI systems placed on the market / put
   into service **before 2 August 2026** get until **2 December 2026** to comply with machine-readable
   marking and detection.
3. **New prohibition** added to the AI Act on AI practices generating **non-consensual sexual/intimate
   content or CSAM** (per Council press release; see "Could NOT verify" for the article number).
4. **Regulatory sandboxes**: national establishment deadline postponed to **2 August 2027**.

## What the Omnibus did NOT change

- **Article 50(1)** — chatbot / AI-interaction disclosure: applies **2 August 2026**.
- **Article 50(3)**, **50(4)**, **50(5)** — unchanged as to date.
- Art 50 applies **regardless of risk classification**; it is not a high-risk obligation, so the
  high-risk deferral does not touch it.
- No grandfathering for systems already on the market as to Art 50(1): per the Commission's para
  (141), compliance is required *"regardless of their date of placement on the market"*.

## Relevance to a companion-eval platform

- The headline "EU delayed the AI Act" is **false as applied to companion chatbots**. The obligation
  that actually bites a companion bot — Art 50(1)/(5) disclosure — is on the original timetable,
  **2 August 2026, i.e. ~2.5 weeks from this research date**.
- Customers with generative voice/image companions get until 2 December 2026 for Art 50(2)
  marking **only if the system was on the market before 2 August 2026**.
- The new NCII/CSAM prohibition is a prohibited-practice (Art 5-type) exposure, which carries the
  higher fine tier — relevant to any companion product with sexual roleplay.
