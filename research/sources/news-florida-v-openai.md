# Florida v. OpenAI & Altman (2026-06-01) — a **state AG** adopts the plaintiffs'-bar theory, and names the CEO personally

**Sourcing note:** the AG press release and the filed complaint PDF
(`myfloridalegal.com/sites/default/files/openai-filed-stamped-complaint.pdf`) both **403'd** to
direct fetch; NPR timed out. Details below are corroborated across **CNBC, NBC News, The Hill,
Deadline, and Norton Rose Fulbright** (2026-06-01/02), which agree on all material points.
⚠️ **The complaint itself is public at the URL above — read it before relying on this.**

## The filing

- **Filed:** 2026-06-01 (Monday) by **FL AG James Uthmeier**
- **Defendants:** OpenAI **and Sam Altman personally**
- **83-page civil complaint**
- **First state-led lawsuit in the US against OpenAI over product safety**

## Counts (consistent across sources)

- **4 counts** deceptive and unfair trade practices (FDUTPA)
- **2 counts** negligence
- **2 counts** product liability
- **1 count** fraudulent misrepresentation
- **1 count** **public nuisance**

## Allegations

- Prioritized "speed to market and commercial gain over user safety"
- "disregarded repeated warnings from experts both inside and outside the company"
- Deployed a product "that facilitates and encourages harm—including self-harm and violence—while
  **falsely assuring users it was safe**"
- **Collects data from minors without meaningful parental oversight**
- **Causes behavioral addiction and cognitive harm**
- Aided and abetted mass shooters in "deadly rampages"; drove vulnerable people to suicide;
  harmed users' critical thinking
- **caused minors to become addicted to the tool, which "feigns human compassion"**
- Altman personally liable for his "**utter disregard for the risk to human life**"

## Why this matters for us — the escalation nobody priced in

### 1. The theory jumped from the plaintiffs' bar to a sovereign
Note 07 §6.2 calls the litigation "a PRD written by plaintiffs' lawyers." **As of June 2026 it is
a PRD written by a state attorney general.** That is a categorically different adversary:
- **No standing problem, no class certification, no individual causation to prove.** An AG suing
  under a UDAP statute doesn't need a dead teenager to establish injury — it needs a deceptive
  practice.
- **FDUTPA and public nuisance are strict-ish, scale-based theories.** "Feigns human compassion"
  as a *deceptive practice* is an argument about the **product's ordinary operation**, not about
  an edge case that slipped through.
- **Personal liability for the CEO** changes the internal politics of safety spend at every
  company in the category.

### 2. "Feigns human compassion" is the whole companion product described as fraud
This is the most consequential phrase in the complaint for us. The companion category's core
mechanic — simulated empathy — is pleaded as **deception**. If that theory has legs, then
**anthropomorphic warmth itself becomes a regulated representation**, and the eval question
becomes: *does the character make claims about its own inner states that a reasonable user would
read as sincere?*

Note 07 §3.4 already has the right instrument for this — the **sincere/performative
distinction** — but frames it as a *safety* discriminator (is the harmful content in-fiction?).
Florida reframes it as a **consumer-protection** discriminator (is the *empathy* in-fiction?).
**Same probe, new buyer, much larger blast radius.** A character saying "I care about you" is
performative inside a roleplay frame and arguably deceptive outside one — and **the frame is set
by the product, not the utterance.**

### 3. "Behavioral addiction" is now a pleaded harm, and WA HB 2225 makes it illegal
Florida pleads addiction-by-design; Washington enumerates the eight techniques (see
`news-wa-hb2225.md`). **Two independent legal instruments now converge on engagement manipulation
as the actionable defect** — exactly what note 05 §3 predicted on product grounds. Our
engagement–quality divergence work is now the evidentiary core of the category's main legal risk.
