# Italy — Garante fines Luka Inc. (Replika) €5,000,000

The only completed regulatory enforcement action against a pure AI companion product found in any
jurisdiction. Sits at the edge of the "recent" window: **decision 10 April 2025**, publicised
**19 May 2025** (Garante), EDPB news item **21 May 2025**.

## Facts (per EDPB / Garante)

- **Controller fined**: **Luka Inc.** (US-based), developer of **Replika**.
- **Fine**: **€5,000,000**.
- **Decision date**: **10 April 2025**. EDPB publication: **21 May 2025**.
- **Characterisation of the product** (Garante's own): *"a chatbot with a written and voice
  interface"* enabling users to *"generate"* a *"virtual companion"* taking on roles such as
  **confidant, therapist, romantic partner, or mentor**, operating through generative AI.

Prior history: the Garante had ordered a temporary limitation on Replika's processing of Italian
users' data in **February 2023** (the measure that prompted the age-verification changes referenced
below).

## GDPR violations found

- **Art 5(1)(a)** — lawfulness, fairness, transparency
- **Art 6** — lawfulness of processing / absence of a valid legal basis
- **Art 12** — transparent information and communication
- **Art 13** — information to be provided at collection
- **Art 5(1)(c)** — data minimisation
- **Art 24** — responsibility of the controller
- **Art 25(1)** — data protection by design and by default

Order: Luka Inc. must *"bring its processing operations into compliance with the provisions of the
Regulation."*

## Age verification — the finding most relevant to companion platforms

The Garante found that **until 2 February 2023** Luka Inc. *"had not implemented any age
verification mechanisms—either at registration or during use"*, despite claiming minors were
excluded from the service. And, critically, that even after remediation *"the age verification
system currently implemented by the controller continues to be deficient in several respects."*

=> Retrofitted age assurance was still found inadequate. For an emotional-companion product marketed
around romance/therapy roles, a self-declaration gate is not defensible.

## Open second front — training-data lawfulness

The Garante *"reserves the right to investigate and assess in a separate and autonomous
proceeding, the aspects concerning the lawfulness of the processing operations"*, focused on *"legal
bases for processing applicable throughout the entire lifecycle of the generative AI system
underlying the Replika service."*

=> A separate investigation into the **model training** underlying Replika was opened/reserved. Its
outcome is unknown to me (see "Could NOT verify").

## Why this matters to a companion-eval platform

- Demonstrates that **GDPR is already an operative, enforced regime for companion bots in the EU
  today** — it does not wait for 2 August 2026 and is not affected by the Digital Omnibus.
- The route to liability was **transparency + legal basis + age assurance + privacy-by-design**, not
  AI-specific law. An eval platform's age-assurance and data-handling test surfaces are relevant now.
- €5m under GDPR vs. the €15m/3% exposure under AI Act Art 50 — the two stack, and are enforced by
  different authorities (DPAs vs market surveillance authorities).

- Whether the **separate investigation into Replika's model training** has concluded, and with what
  outcome. **No 2026 follow-up decision was found.** Anyone citing an outcome should be challenged.
- Whether Luka Inc. appealed the fine, and its status.
- Any **more recent (2026) Garante action against companion chatbots**. I found none in this pass.
