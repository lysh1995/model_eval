# Model collapse — the feedback-contamination bound

Nature 2024. Supplemented by https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Model_collapse (for the
subsequent debate) and the critique at https://arxiv.org/html/2410.12954v2.

## Definition

Degradation of a generative model trained on synthetic data / outputs of previous model
versions. "Indiscriminate use of model-generated content in training causes **irreversible
defects** in the resulting models, in which **tails of the original content distribution
disappear**."

Affects LLMs, VAEs, and Gaussian mixture models — i.e. it is a property of the recursive
estimation procedure, not of transformers.

## Two stages — and the first one is the dangerous one for us

**Early collapse:** the model "**loses information about distribution tails**", primarily
affecting **minority data**. **Performance metrics may appear stable while minority-data
accuracy declines significantly.**

**Late collapse:** substantial performance loss; "concepts become confused and most
variance is lost."

> **"Aggregate metrics look fine while the tail dies" is precisely our platform's failure
> mode, restated.** Note 11's Lane 2 exists because *voice homogenization is invisible
> per-character and only measurable across the cohort*; note 06 §3 exists because *uniform
> sampling starves the tail*. Early model collapse is the same shape: **the mean is
> preserved, the tail is destroyed, and any monitor watching the mean reports green.**
>
> Our 95-character grid **is** the tail. A companion product whose deep-tail characters
> homogenize into the head voice is early-stage collapse — and note 09 already measured
> homogenization as a real, moving quantity. This is not a hypothetical risk for us.

## Mechanism — three compounding error sources

1. **Statistical approximation error** — finite sampling; tails are lost first because they
   are rare (this is the dominant one and it is unavoidable with finite samples)
2. **Functional expressivity error** — model family can't represent the true distribution
3. **Functional approximation error** — learning procedure / optimizer limits

## The math (1-D Gaussian, constant sample size M)

Variance diverges **linearly** in generations, random-walk behaviour:

```
Var(X^n) = σ²(1 + n/M)
```

"Even increasing sampling superlinearly cannot fully prevent distance from the original
distribution." For a recursively-estimated Gaussian, variance eventually **converges to
zero — collapsing the distribution into a delta function at a single point.**

## The critical debate — replace vs ACCUMULATE

The Nature result assumes each generation **replaces** its training data with the previous
model's output. Subsequent work (see Wikipedia summary; Gerstgrasser et al.) shows:

> "**model collapse is avoidable when synthetic and human data accumulate together**,
> arguing realistic scenarios involve data growth rather than replacement."

Also see the critique note (arXiv 2410.12954) questioning the framing.

> **This is the actionable finding, and it is a schema requirement, not a philosophy.**
> Collapse is driven by *replacement*. If the human-authored data stays in the pool and
> synthetic accumulates alongside it, the degeneration is largely averted.
>
> For our platform the analogue is exact and concrete:
>
> **The benchmark must ACCUMULATE, never REPLACE.** When we mine production and promote
> hard cases into the regression suite (the flywheel), those cases are **generated by our
> own variants**. If curation gradually replaces the human-authored anchor set with
> model-generated cases, we are running the Nature experiment on our own eval set —
> generation by generation the benchmark's tails vanish, and the benchmark converges to
> "whatever our models already do." The eval would report improvement forever, by
> construction.
>
> Three rules follow:
> 1. **The frozen human-authored anchor set (note 11 §3) is never regenerated and never
>    retired.** It is the "human data" term that makes accumulation safe. It is also
>    already load-bearing for pairwise comparability — the same artifact serves both.
> 2. **Every dataset item carries its provenance** (`human_authored` vs `mined_from_prod`)
>    and mined items are **capped as a fraction of the suite**, monitored as a first-class
>    number. When that ratio drifts toward 1.0, the eval set is collapsing.
> 3. **Never judge with a model from the same family that generated the item, and never
>    promote items selected *by* the judge alone** — that is the recursive loop with extra
>    steps. Note 11's family-disjoint panel already implements half of this; provenance
>    tagging implements the other half.

## Mitigations (from the literature)

- Data filtering and **watermarking to exclude synthetic content**
- **Entropy regularization** during fine-tuning to preserve output diversity
- Maintaining **diverse model ecosystems** rather than one system
- Machine-generated text detection to prevent collapse (arXiv 2502.15654)

> Note the mitigations are all **provenance mechanisms**. You cannot fix collapse
> statistically after the fact; you fix it by **knowing which data is model-generated**.
> That is a lineage field, and it must exist from day one — it cannot be backfilled.
