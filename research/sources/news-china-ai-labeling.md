# China — AI-Generated Synthetic Content Labeling Measures (effective 1 September 2025)

## Scope

Art 1 basis: Cybersecurity Law; Provisions on the Management of Algorithmic Recommendations in
Internet Information Services; Provisions on the Administration of Deep Synthesis Internet
Information Services; Interim Measures for the Management of Generative AI Services.

Art 2: applies to labeling by online information service providers falling within those three
instruments ("service providers").

Art 3: *"'AI-generated synthetic content' refers to text, images, audio, video, virtual scenes, or
other information that is generated or synthesized using AI technology."* Labeling is of two kinds:

- **Explicit labels** (显式标识): *"labels added to generated synthetic content **or interactive
  scenario interfaces**, which appear in forms such as text, sound, or images, and can be clearly
  perceived by users."*
- **Implicit labels** (隐式标识): *"labels added to generated synthetic content file data by
  employing technical measures, which are not easily perceived or known of by users."*

Note that "interactive scenario interfaces" (交互场景界面) is in the definition of explicit label —
this is the chatbot hook.

## Article 4 — Explicit labeling (applies where the service falls within Art 17 §1 of the Deep Synthesis Provisions)

1. **Text**: *"Add labels such as text notifications, or notifications using common symbols, at
   appropriate positions in the beginning, end, or middle of text, or add conspicuous notification
   labels **in interactive scenario interfaces or beside text**"* (在交互场景界面、文字周边添加显著的提示标识).
2. **Audio**: voice notifications or audio/rhythmic notifications at beginning/end/middle, or
   conspicuous notification labels in interactive scenario interfaces.
3. **Images**: conspicuous notification labels at appropriate positions.
4. **Video**: conspicuous labels on the starting screen and around the playback area; optionally
   end/middle.
5. **Virtual scenes**: conspicuous labels on the starting screen; optionally during ongoing service.
6. Catch-all: *"Explicit labels that are effective in giving conspicuous notice should be added in
   other generation and synthesis service scenarios based on the characteristics of the
   applications."*

Art 4 final ¶: download/copy/export functions must ensure the **file** contains conforming explicit
labels.

## Article 5 — Implicit labeling

Implicit labels must be added to **file metadata** per Art 16 of the Deep Synthesis Provisions, and
must include: *"the generated synthetic content's attribute information, the service providers' name
or code, and the content reference number."* Digital watermarks are **encouraged**, not mandated.
*"'File Metadata' refers to descriptive information embedded in the file header according to a
specific encoding format."*

## Article 6 — Distribution platform duties

Platforms transmitting content must: (1) verify metadata for implicit labels and, where present, add
conspicuous labels around published content stating it **is** generated; (2) where absent but the
user declares it generated, label it as **possibly** generated; (3) where absent and undeclared but
the platform detects explicit labels or other traces, label it as **suspected** generated; (4)
provide labeling functions and prompt users to proactively declare. In cases (1)–(3), add
transmission-factor info to metadata (attributes, platform name/code, content number).

## Articles 7–13

- **Art 7**: app distribution platforms must, at listing review, require the app provider to state
  whether it provides generative AI services, and if so **check the labeling-related materials**.
- **Art 8**: labeling methods/styles must be explained in the **user service agreement**.
- **Art 9**: a user may request content **without** explicit labels; the provider may supply it only
  after clarifying the user's labeling obligations and usage responsibility via the agreement, and
  must **retain relevant logs for at least 6 months**.
- **Art 10**: users publishing generated content must proactively declare it and use the platform's
  labeling function. *"The generated synthetic content labels provided for in these Measures must not
  be maliciously deleted, altered, fabricated, or concealed by any organization or individual, tools
  and services must not be provided for others to carry out the malicious conduct described above."*
- **Art 11**: must also comply with mandatory national standards.
- **Art 12**: labeling materials must be provided when performing **algorithm filing or security
  assessment** procedures.
- **Art 13**: violations handled by internet information, telecommunications, public security, and
  radio/TV authorities under applicable law (no standalone fine schedule in these Measures).

## Relevance to a companion-eval platform

- Binds **customers** offering generative AI services in China. Art 4(1)'s "interactive scenario
  interface" labeling is the text/chat hook; Art 5's metadata labeling is the analogue of EU AI Act
  Art 50(2).
- Cross-reference: **Art 18 of the AI Anthropomorphic Interactive Services Measures (effective 15
  July 2026)** expressly requires companion providers to *"fulfil the AI-generated synthetic content
  labelling obligations"* — i.e. these Measures are incorporated by reference into the companion-bot
  regime. See news-china-ai-companion-measures.md.
- Testable: presence/persistence of explicit in-interface labeling; metadata integrity on
  export/download; label robustness.
