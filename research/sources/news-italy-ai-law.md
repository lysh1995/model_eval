# Italy — Law No. 132/2025 (in force 10 October 2025): under-14 parental consent for AI access

Included because of one provision with direct companion-bot relevance: an **age-of-consent rule for
access to AI itself**, which has no EU AI Act equivalent.

**CAUTION: this file is built from law-firm analyses, not the Gazzetta Ufficiale text. The URL above
is the conventional GU citation and was NOT retrieved/verified in this pass. Treat the detail as
corroborated-but-unverified.**

## Dates (consistent across multiple independent law-firm sources)

- Approved by the Italian Parliament: **17 September 2025**.
- Law dated: **23 September 2025** (Law No. 132/2025).
- Published in the **Gazzetta Ufficiale**: **25 September 2025**.
- **In force: 10 October 2025** (after the standard 15-day vacatio legis).
- Structure: **28 articles**. Billed as *"the first domestic law in the European Union on the use of
  artificial intelligence"*, drafted to sit alongside (not replace) Regulation (EU) 2024/1689.

## The provision that matters here — minors' access to AI

- **Under 14**: access to AI technologies **requires the consent of the person exercising parental
  responsibility**.
- **14–18**: a minor who has reached 14 **may give their own consent** to the processing of personal
  data relating to the use of AI systems, **provided the information and communications are easily
  accessible and understandable**.

=> In Italy, a companion platform must gate under-14s behind verified parental consent as of
**10 October 2025** — earlier than, and independent of, EU AI Act Art 50 (2 August 2026).

Note the convergence: Italy (under-14 parental consent, Oct 2025) and China (under-14 guardian
consent + outright ban on virtual partners for minors, July 2026) landed on the **same age
threshold** from opposite legal traditions. GDPR Art 8 also uses 13–16 as the member-state range for
information-society-service consent, with Italy having set 14.

## Scope beyond minors

The law sets principles for AI use across sectors — healthcare, employment, professional services,
intellectual property, public administration — and contains delegations (deleghe) empowering the
Government to adopt further implementing legislation. Nothing found addresses companion/roleplay
bots specifically.

## Relevance to a companion-eval platform

- Binds **customers** serving Italian users. Adds an age-gating obligation on top of GDPR and the
  AI Act.
- Combined with the Garante's Replika decision (see news-italy-garante-replika.md), Italy is the
  most aggressive single EU member state for this product category: a €5m fine already levied against
  a companion app, plus a national statute requiring parental consent for under-14 AI access.
- Testable: age gate at under-14 with parental-consent verification; comprehensibility of notices to
  14–17 year-olds ("easily accessible and understandable").
