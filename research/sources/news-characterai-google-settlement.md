# Character.AI / Google settle the teen-harm docket (Jan 7–8, 2026)

**Sourcing note:** CNN and CBS were 403/451 to direct fetch; the settlement is corroborated
across CNN, CNBC, CBS News, Fortune (fetched, Jan 8 2026), and K-12 Dive. Fortune was fetched
directly and is the strongest verified source here. **Settlement terms are confidential** — any
source claiming to know the dollar figure is not credible.

## What happened

- **2026-01-07** — Google and Character Technologies disclosed in a **court filing** that they had
  reached a **mediated settlement** with the family of Sewell Setzer III. Filed in the **U.S.
  District Court, Middle District of Florida** (Garcia v. Character Technologies, 6:24-cv-01903).
- Reporting indicates **Judge Conway** issued a settlement order on **Jan 7** with **90 days to
  finalize terms**. ⚠️ *The 90-day figure appears in secondary reporting; I could not fetch the
  docket to confirm. Treat as probable, not confirmed.*
- **Five cases** settled in total, across **Florida, Colorado, New York, and Texas**. Defendants
  include Character Technologies, founders **Noam Shazeer and Daniel De Freitas**, and **Google**.

## The critical facts

> "settlement in principle" — specific details have not been disclosed, and **no admission of
> liability** appears in the filings. (Fortune, 2026-01-08)

- **Terms confidential.** No disclosed financial figure. No disclosed safety commitments *as
  settlement terms*.
- **No admission of liability.**
- Claims pleaded across the five cases: **negligence, wrongful death, deceptive trade practices,
  product liability**.

## ⚠️ Correction of a widespread aggregator error

Several secondary sources (e.g. openclassactions.com, aicerts.ai) state or imply that
Character.AI's **"AI Safety Lab"** nonprofit was a *settlement commitment*. **It was not.**
The AI Safety Lab was announced by Character.AI on **2025-11-25**, in its own under-18
announcement — six weeks *before* the settlement filing, and independent of it. Do not cite it
as a settlement term.

## Why this matters for us — the precedent that DIDN'T happen

**This is the most important consequence and it is a negative one.**

Note 07 §6.2 treats Garcia as **live litigation** that would generate a merits precedent. It will
not. The settlement means:

1. **The May 2025 MTD ruling is now the ceiling.** Judge Conway's May 2025 order — which declined
   to treat LLM output as protected speech at the pleading stage and allowed product-liability
   theories to proceed — **stands as the only judicial word**, and it is a *motion-to-dismiss*
   ruling, not a liability finding. It was never tested at trial. It is persuasive, not settled.
2. **No damages number was ever set by a factfinder.** The engagement-optimization-as-defect
   theory has never been priced by a jury. Anyone modelling liability exposure from Garcia is
   modelling from a confidential settlement, i.e. from nothing.
3. **The theory survives and migrates.** Settling removed the risk of an *adverse* precedent for
   plaintiffs too. The same defective-design theory is now being run in Raine (OpenAI), Soelberg,
   Peralta, Gavalas v. Google (Gemini), and — significantly — by a **state AG** (Florida, June
   2026, see `news-florida-v-openai.md`). Settlement did not kill the theory; it validated it as
   worth paying to avoid.
4. **Defendants pay rather than litigate.** Five cases, two corporate defendants plus two founders
   personally, resolved simultaneously and quietly. That is the behavior of parties who did not
   want discovery or a verdict on the record. For a vendor selling safety evals, the buying signal
   is: *the cost of not having the receipts is high enough to settle.*

**Design implication:** our value proposition cannot be "we help you win Garcia-style cases."
It is "we generate the contemporaneous, auditable record that makes the case settleable cheaply —
or never filed." The artifact that matters is the **documented detection→escalation trail**, not
