# Vietnam — Law on Artificial Intelligence (in force 1 March 2026)

**LOW CONFIDENCE FILE — secondary sources only.** I could not retrieve the statutory text
(thuvienphapluat.vn and luatvietnam return 403 to automated requests) and found no Library of
Congress Global Legal Monitor article for this law. Included for completeness because it is a recent
non-US comprehensive AI law with a reported chatbot-disclosure duty. **Verify before any reliance.**

## What is reasonably well corroborated (consistent across several independent law-firm sources)

- Passed by the **National Assembly on 10 December 2025**; **Law No. 134/2025/QH15**.
- **In force 1 March 2026.**
- Vietnam's **first comprehensive standalone AI law**; reported as *"draw[ing] considerable influence
  from the European Union's Artificial Intelligence Act."*
- **35 articles, 8 chapters**, covering research, development, provision, deployment and use of AI
  systems.
- **Three risk tiers** (reported as Article 9): **high, medium, low** — based on potential impact on
  human rights, safety, security and public interests.
- Replaces/supersedes AI provisions previously in the Law on Digital Technology Industry.
- **Grace periods**: full compliance by **1 March 2027** for most sectors (12 months); by
  **1 September 2027** for AI systems in **finance, healthcare and education** (18 months).

## Reported chatbot-relevant obligations — NOT VERIFIED

Secondary summaries state:
- **Chatbots are classified as medium-risk** systems (as systems with *"the potential to cause
  confusion or manipulate user perception"*).
- *"Users must be told immediately if they are talking to a chatbot."*
- AI-generated photos, videos and audio must carry machine-detectable **hidden tags**.
- AI content resembling a real person or event must be **clearly labelled**.
- Generative AI providers must **disclose training data sources** and apply **digital watermarks**.

**None of these is verified against the statute.** The article numbers are unknown to me. The
training-data-source disclosure duty in particular would be unusual and stricter than the EU AI Act
for non-GPAI systems — treat with suspicion until confirmed.

## Relevance to a companion-eval platform

- Potentially binds **customers** with Vietnamese users from 1 March 2026, with grace to 1 March 2027.
- If the "medium-risk chatbot + immediate disclosure" framing is accurate, the testable surface
  mirrors EU Art 50(1). But do not build customer guidance on this file.
