# South Korea — AI Basic Act / AI Framework Act (in force 22 January 2026)

Primary-adjacent source: the **Law Library of Congress** Global Legal Monitor (authoritative,
cites article numbers). Corroborated by Future of Privacy Forum and law-firm analyses. **I did not
retrieve the statutory text from law.go.kr** — see "Could NOT verify".

## Identity and dates (verified via LOC)

- Full name: **Act on the Development of Artificial Intelligence and the Establishment of a
  Foundation for Trust** (aka AI Basic Act / AI Framework Act). Korean: 인공지능 발전과 신뢰 기반
  조성 등에 관한 기본법.
- **Act No. 20676, 21 January 2025** (promulgated).
- **In force: 22 January 2026** — one year after promulgation.
- **Enforcement Decree: Presidential Decree No. 36053, 21 January 2026**, took effect the same day
  (22 January 2026).
- Competent authority: **Minister of Science and ICT (MSIT)** (Art 6). National AI Basic Plan
  (Arts 7–8).
- It is a **framework statute** — *"with detailed implementation delegated to subordinate
  legislation, principally the Enforcement Decree"*.

This is the world's second comprehensive AI statute after the EU AI Act.

## Article 31 — Transparency (the provision that binds chatbots)

Per LOC: *"Operators providing products or services using generative AI or high-impact AI are
required to inform users in advance that AI is being used and, where AI-generated outputs may be
difficult to distinguish from non-AI content, to label or otherwise indicate that such outputs are
AI-generated. (Enforcement Decree, art. 23.)"*

Sub-structure (from FPF and corroborating analyses — **not** verified against statutory text):
- **Art 31(1)**: operators offering high-impact **or generative** AI products/services must *"notify
  users in advance that the product or service utilizes AI."*
- **Art 31(2)**: generative AI providers must *"indicate that output generated was generated by
  generative AI."*
- **Art 31(3)**: where an AI system provides virtual sounds, images, video or other content
  *"difficult to distinguish from reality"*, the operator must *"notify or display the fact that the
  result was generated by an (AI) system in a manner that allows users to clearly recognize it"* —
  with flexibility so as not to hinder creative expression or appreciation.
- Implementation details (how notification/labelling is done) are prescribed by the **Enforcement
  Decree, Art 23**.

**Application to companion bots**: a companion/roleplay chatbot is **generative AI**, so Art 31(1)
(advance notice that the service uses AI) and Art 31(2) (indicate output is generative-AI-generated)
bite. Art 31(3) bites for voice/image/video companions.

## High-impact AI — companion bots are probably NOT caught

Definition (Art 2): AI whose use *"may have a significant effect on human life, safety, or
fundamental rights."* The enumerated domains reported are: **energy; healthcare; nuclear operations;
biometric data analysis; public decision-making; education**; plus further areas addable by
Presidential Decree.

Duties if high-impact: operators must **assess whether a system qualifies as high-impact prior to
deployment** and may seek confirmation from the competent authority (Art 33); enhanced obligations
for safety, reliability and human oversight, including risk management and control measures
(Art 34); impact assessments on fundamental rights and public interests (Art 35).

=> **Companionship/roleplay is not on the enumerated list.** A companion bot marketed for emotional
support could arguably touch "healthcare" or fundamental rights, but I found **no** authority
placing companion bots in the high-impact category. Treat as: generative-AI transparency duties
apply; high-impact duties do not, absent a Presidential Decree addition or a health-adjacent
positioning.

## Article 32 — Frontier compute threshold (not applicable to most companion platforms)

Operators must identify/assess/mitigate risks for AI trained above a compute threshold. Enforcement
Decree Art 24 sets it at **≥10²⁶ floating-point operations**, plus incorporating state-of-the-art AI
techniques and presenting material risk to life, safety or fundamental rights. (LOC notes this
mirrors the threshold in US EO 14110 of 30 Oct 2023.)

=> Binds frontier model developers, not a companion app built on someone else's model.

## Article 36 — Domestic representative (extraterritorial hook)

*"[T]he act ... requires AI business operators without a domestic address or place of business to
designate a domestic agent, if statutory conditions are met."* Thresholds are prescribed
(user-number or revenue based).

**Art 4(1)** (per FPF): the Act applies to acts done abroad that *"impact South Korea's domestic
market, or users in South Korea."* => A foreign companion platform with Korean users is in scope.

## Enforcement and penalties

- Supervision and administrative measures including **corrective orders** (Arts 38–40).
- **Administrative fines up to KRW 30 million** (~USD 21,000) — reported as Art 43 — for: failure to
  designate a domestic representative; **failure to notify users that they are interacting with a
  generative or high-impact AI system**; and failure to comply with a corrective order.
- **Grace period**: LOC — *"the government is implementing the AI Basic Act with a grace period of at
  least one year before imposing administrative fines."* MSIT reportedly defers fact-finding
  investigations and fines during 2026 except in exceptional cases involving serious social harm
  (e.g. loss of life or human-rights violations).
- MSIT runs a legal support desk (reported: via the Korea Software Industry Association) and has
  issued/plans draft guidelines (high-impact determination, business-operator obligations, impact
  assessments, generative AI transparency, advanced AI safety), posted on the National Information
  Society Agency website.

## Relevance to a companion-eval platform

- Binds **customers** with Korean users, from 22 January 2026, but the fine exposure is small
  (KRW 30m) and deferred by a ≥1-year grace period running through roughly January 2027.
- Concretely testable: advance AI-use notice; per-output generative-AI indication; clear labelling of
  realistic synthetic audio/image/video.
- Practically, Korea is the **weakest** of the five regimes for companion bots: transparency-only,
  small fines, grace period, and companion bots fall outside high-impact AI. Do not oversell it.
