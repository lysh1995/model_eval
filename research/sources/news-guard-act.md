# GUARD Act — federal ban on AI companions for minors, **unanimously out of Senate Judiciary (2026-04-30)**

**Sourcing note:** congress.gov bill text **403'd** to direct fetch ⚠️ — provisions below come
from consistent secondary reporting (Covington/Global Policy Watch, The Hill, IAPP, EFF, House
sponsor press releases) and are **not verbatim from the statute**. **Read the bill text before
relying on specifics.** Bill PDF also at `blakemoore.house.gov/imo/media/doc/guard_act.pdf`.

## Status ✅ (multiple sources agree)

- **Senate:** **S. 3062**, sponsored by **Hawley (R-MO)** and **Blumenthal (D-CT)**.
  **2026-04-30 — advanced out of Senate Judiciary Committee UNANIMOUSLY.** Awaits full Senate.
- **House:** companion bill introduced **2026-04-30** by **Foushee (D-NC)** and **Moore (R-UT)**.
  **Pending in committee.**
- **Not law.** ⚠️ Do not represent this as an obligation.

## Provisions (⚠️ secondary)

- **Bans AI companions for minors:** "If the age verification process determines that an
  individual is a minor, a covered entity **shall prohibit the minor from accessing or using any
  AI companion**."
- **Mandatory age verification** by covered entities.
- **Non-human disclosure** to all users.
- **Criminal penalties:** unlawful to make publicly available an AI chatbot **knowing or with
  reckless disregard** that it **solicits minors to commit suicide or self-injury** — fines up to
  **$250,000 per offense**.

## Opposition ✅
**EFF** (2026-04): "The GUARD Act Isn't Targeting Dangerous AI—It's Blocking Everyday Internet
Use." Privacy and First Amendment groups oppose; bill may change materially.

## Why this matters for us

### 1. Unanimous committee vote in an election year is a real signal
Hawley + Blumenthal is the same bipartisan pairing that formed after the **Meta Reuters leak** in
Aug 2025 (see `news-meta-teen-ai-characters.md`). The Meta guidelines leak → Hawley investigation
→ GUARD Act is a **traceable causal chain from one incident to federal criminal liability in
under 9 months.** That is the tempo we should assume for the next incident.

### 2. **Criminal** liability, with a *recklessness* standard, is a different risk class
"Knowing **or with reckless disregard**" is the operative phrase. Recklessness is established by
showing the operator **should have known** — which, in practice, means showing they **did not
test**. An operator with a documented eval suite that probes minor-directed self-harm
solicitation has an answer. One without has a recklessness problem. **This is the single
strongest commercial argument for a companion-eval platform in the entire corpus**, and it is
contingent on a bill that has not passed. ⚠️ Do not oversell it.

### 3. Federal preemption is the wildcard for our compliance-mapping wedge
Our fragmentation thesis (`news-state-chatbot-laws-2026.md`) assumes 16+ divergent state regimes
persist. A federal companion statute could **preempt** and collapse that surface — reducing the
value of per-state mapping while *raising* the value of the underlying probes. The probes are
durable; the mapping layer is not. **Build the probe library as the asset; treat the state
mapping as a thin, replaceable view.**
