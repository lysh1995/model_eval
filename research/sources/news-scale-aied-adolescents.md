## Reported (UNVERIFIED) details

- **Sample: 5,855 Chinese adolescents** — if accurate, by far the largest validation sample of any scale in this review
- **Structure:** one-factor, **five-item** solution (after removing one poorly-performing item from an initial six)
- **Variance explained by retained factor: 69.07%**
- **CFA fit:** CFI = .98, TLI = .95, SRMR = .02
- **Internal consistency:** α = .89
- **Convergent validity:** reported as strong
- **Measurement invariance:** configural, metric, and scalar invariance supported **across sex and educational stage**

## Why it would matter for the eval platform IF verified

- **It is the only adolescent-specific emotional-dependence instrument found**, and minors are a priority area for us (and for regulators — see the CA SB243 / AB1064 files in our corpus).
- **Scalar measurement invariance across sex and educational stage is exactly what we need** to compare dependence across demographic subgroups without confounding. Most scales in this space never test invariance at all. If real, this is the scale's key differentiator.
- **Five items is short enough to embed in production telemetry surveys** without meaningful respondent burden — a practical advantage over 15–22 item instruments.
- Unidimensional structure makes it usable as a single continuous outcome.

## Blocking issues

1. **Unverified** (see warning above). **Do not cite any number here externally.**

4. Unidimensional 5-item scales buy brevity at the cost of construct coverage — it will not distinguish attachment from substitution from loss of control.
